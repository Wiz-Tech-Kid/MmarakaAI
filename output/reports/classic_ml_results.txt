WHAT THE NUMBERS MEAN

RMSE 4.2954:
This measures prediction error and penalizes large errors more heavily. Lower is better. The model's typical error is roughly 4.30 target units under this measure.

MAE 2.8506:
This is the average absolute prediction error. Predictions were off by about 2.85 target units on average in the held-out test period.

R-squared 0.2720:
This compares the model with a baseline that always predicts the average value. A value of 1 is perfect, 0 means no improvement over that baseline, and a negative value is worse than that baseline. This model explains about 27.2% of the variation in the held-out period, which is useful signal but still a modest forecasting result.

GENERALIZATION
The test RMSE of 4.2954 is higher than the cross-validation RMSE of 3.3555. The latest test period was harder than the earlier validation periods. This may reflect changing economic conditions or some overfitting to earlier periods.
