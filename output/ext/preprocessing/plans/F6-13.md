Purpose of this document:

This document defines the engineering specification for preprocessing the F6-13 dataset before it enters merge.py and the downstream modelling workflow.

Unlike the analysis report, which only summarizes the spreadsheet characteristics, this document specifies every preprocessing operation that preprocessing.py shall implement. The objective is to transform the F6-13 statistical publication into a standardized monthly analytical dataset that can be merged consistently with every other economic dataset within the MmarakaAI pipeline.

This preprocessing stage prepares the dataset exclusively for integration into the modelling pipeline. Feature engineering, lag generation, rolling statistics, scaling, normalization, encoding and machine learning transformations are intentionally excluded.

# Dataset Overview

The F6-13 workbook follows the typical structure of Botswana government statistical publications.

The workbook commonly contains

• publication titles

• report identifiers

• reporting periods

• explanatory notes

• merged cells

• blank separator rows

• blank separator columns

• repeated headers

• statistical observations

These formatting components improve readability but reduce analytical usability.

The preprocessing objective is therefore to reconstruct the publication into a normalized monthly dataset suitable for merge.py.

The workbook should never be treated as a machine-learning-ready dataset before structural transformation has been completed.

# Structural Changes

## 1. Publication Reconstruction

The workbook shall initially be treated as a publication document rather than an analytical dataset.

preprocessing.py shall remove

• publication titles

• report numbers

• explanatory paragraphs

• notes

• footnotes

• source references

• copyright statements

• blank rows

• blank columns

• repeated section headers

• merged formatting cells

These elements are formatting artefacts and contain no analytical value.

Structural reconstruction must always precede data cleaning.

---

## 2. Header Detection

The imported dataframe may contain numerous unnamed columns resulting from spreadsheet formatting.

The preprocessing pipeline shall automatically locate the first row containing the actual analytical variables.

That row shall become the dataframe header.

Rows above this point shall be discarded.

Remaining unnamed columns shall be removed only after verifying that they contain no analytical observations.

---

## 3. Wide-to-Long Transformation

The publication stores observations across multiple columns.

Illustrative example

```
Category        Jan     Feb     Mar     Apr
Food
Beverages
Cereals
Sugar
Dairy
```

This layout is not compatible with merge.py.

The preprocessing pipeline shall reshape the publication into

Date

Category

Value

Each row shall represent one category measured during one month.

This normalized structure becomes the standard representation throughout MmarakaAI.

---

## 4. Cleaning

Cleaning begins only after publication reconstruction has completed.

Cleaning operations include

• remove duplicated observations

• remove repeated publication headers

• trim whitespace

• standardize category names

• validate numerical values

• remove empty analytical rows

Formatting artefacts shall never be interpreted as analytical observations.

---

## 5. Datetime Normalization

Monthly reporting periods shall be converted into pandas datetime format.

Examples

Jan-2020

↓

2020-01-01

2020M01

↓

2020-01-01

2020-01

↓

2020-01-01

Every monthly observation shall be represented by the first day of its month.

This standardization enables direct integration with all remaining monthly datasets.

---

## 6. Domain Filtering

Only variables relevant to Botswana food inflation modelling shall be retained.

Depending on the final analytical structure, preprocessing shall preserve categories such as

Food

Food Products

Food Manufacturing

Beverages

Agricultural Products

Consumer Food Categories

Imported Food Categories

Food Commodities

Variables unrelated to food markets should be removed where appropriate.

Examples include

Mining

Telecommunications

Construction

Heavy Manufacturing

Public Administration

Insurance

Financial Services

The objective is to maximize predictive relevance while reducing unnecessary dimensionality.

---

## 7. Missing Value Assessment

Missing values identified immediately after importing the spreadsheet shall not automatically be treated as genuine missing observations.

Many missing cells originate from

• merged spreadsheet cells

• publication formatting

• blank separators

• repeated section breaks

Only missing analytical observations remaining after structural reconstruction shall be evaluated.

Automatic imputation shall not occur during preprocessing.
